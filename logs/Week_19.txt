Week 19 — Runtime Confidence Calibration & Correlation Refinement

Hours Logged: 22

Objective

Improve the precision and interpretability of runtime validation by refining correlation scoring and introducing confidence calibration mechanisms.

Activities

Refined the static–runtime correlation engine to improve precision and reduce false-positive confirmations. Introduced score normalization and suppression logic to eliminate weak correlations that lack sufficient execution plan evidence.

Enhanced the correlation scoring model by revisiting rule-specific heuristics, particularly for missing index detection, ORDER BY sorting, and SELECT * scenarios. Adjusted scoring thresholds to better distinguish between strong, moderate, and weak runtime signals.

Implemented suppression of low-confidence matches by introducing a minimum score threshold, ensuring that only meaningful correlations contribute to confirmation metrics. This reduced noise in the evaluation pipeline and improved overall reliability of reported results.

Analyzed execution plan operator distributions across the expanded workload to validate updated scoring behavior. Verified that high-confidence confirmations consistently align with expected execution plan characteristics such as scans, sorts, and join strategies.

Regenerated runtime validation artifacts and rule-level metrics to reflect updated scoring behavior.

Outcome

Correlation precision improved through suppression of weak signals and refined scoring heuristics. Runtime validation results are now more interpretable, with clearer separation between confirmed and unconfirmed warnings.
